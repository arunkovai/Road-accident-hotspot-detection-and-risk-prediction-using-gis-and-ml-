Key Findings & Insights

  • Avinashi Road is currently the most heavily accident-prone area monitored.
  • Overspeeding and Signal Violations remain the most frequent catalyst for critical accidents.
  • Non-fatal accidents and minor scrapes occur substantially more often than fatal crashes.
  • Fatalities and severe risk exponentially increase during Night Time combined with Poor Lighting/Road Conditions.
  • Predictions using predictive ML indicate strict traffic calming measures must be deployed by 2026 to plateau the rising trend.

About Project

Final Year Data Science Project

Purpose of Project

This project aims to analyze historical accident data in Coimbatore city to identify high-risk zones (hotspots) and provide a predictive model that estimates the risk level based on environmental and situational factors.

Importance

Detecting accident hotspots allows traffic authorities and urban planners to deploy targeted interventions such as improved lighting, speed breakers, and better signage to significantly reduce mortality and morbidity on the roads.

GIS & Machine Learning

Integrating Geographic Information Systems (GIS) with Data Science provides spatial context to accident data. Combined with Machine Learning, authorities can shift from reactive measures to proactive risk mitigation.

Machine Learning Model Used

Model: Decision Tree

Purpose: To predict accident risk level

Input Parameters:

  • Location
  • Time of Day
  • Road Condition
  • Traffic Density
  • Reported Issue

Output: Risk Level (Low / Medium / High)
